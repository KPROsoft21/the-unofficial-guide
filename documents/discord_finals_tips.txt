[senior_ta] Reposting my annual finals advice for the freshmen:

1. For CS61A and 61B, the single best prep is redoing past exams under timed conditions. Both classes post past finals. Do not just read solutions — actually sit down for 3 hours and take them cold. The exams reward speed and pattern recognition.

2. For CS70, the proof homework IS the exam prep. If you understood every homework proof, the exam is doable. Make a one-page cheat sheet of proof techniques (induction, contradiction, counting) — handwritten sheets are allowed for CS70 finals.

3. Sleep over cramming. Every year someone pulls an all-nighter and bombs because they can't think. The CS exams test reasoning, not recall.

[student_2024] Adding: the libraries get packed during finals. Moffitt is open 24/7 during RRR week and finals. Main Stacks is quieter. The Engineering Library (Kresge) has the best desks but closes earlier.

[senior_ta] Yes — and the curve will save you more than you think. Median grades in 61B and 70 are usually a B+. A score that feels like failure is often fine after the curve. Don't spiral over one bad exam.
